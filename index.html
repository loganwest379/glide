Things to Know

When should I get a hot wax?

Your gear will tell you! Look for these signs:

  • Feeling slow or 'sticky' on the snow.
  • Base looks dry, white, or chalky (especially near the edges).
  • After several days of riding (3-5 is a good rule of thumb for most).
  • Before storing your gear for the off-season to prevent base drying.

How long will my hot wax last?

It depends on snow conditions, how hard you ride, and your base structure, but a quality hot wax typically lasts 3-5 full ski days. You might still glide after that, but performance and base protection decrease. Regular waxing is key ‚Äì avid riders often get a wax every few weeks.

What's the difference between Edge & Wax and a Pro Tune?

Edge & Wax focuses on grip and glide: sharpening edges and applying hot wax. A Pro Tune is the full overhaul: it includes everything in an Edge & Wax, plus base cleaning and P-tex repairs to fill in scratches/gouges, making your base like new again. ‚ú®
