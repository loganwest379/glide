Things to Know

When should I get a hot wax?

Your gear will tell you! Look for these signs:

  • Feeling slow or 'sticky' on the snow.
  • Base looks dry, white, or chalky (especially near the edges).
  • After several days of riding (3-5 is a good rule of thumb for most).
  • Before storing your gear for the off-season to prevent base drying.
Are there different types of wax?

Yes! Choosing the right wax optimizes glide for the conditions:

Hot Waxes: Penetrate the base for lasting performance. We offer:

  • Cold Temp: For temps below 20°F (dry, squeaky snow). 🥶
  • All-Temp: Our versatile default for typical Utah days (15°F - 30°F). 👍
  • Warm Temp: For spring skiing or wet snow (above 28°F). ☀️

Quick Wax (Rub-On): A fast surface treatment for a temporary glide boost. Great for a quick fix before hitting the slopes, but doesn't nourish or protect the base like a hot wax. ⏱️

How long will my hot wax last?

It depends on snow conditions, how hard you ride, and your base structure, but a quality hot wax typically lasts 3-5 full ski days. You might still glide after that, but performance and base protection decrease. Regular waxing is key – avid riders often get a wax every few weeks.

What's the difference between Edge & Wax and a Pro Tune?

Edge & Wax focuses on grip and glide: sharpening edges and applying hot wax. A Pro Tune is the full overhaul: it includes everything in an Edge & Wax, plus base cleaning and P-tex repairs to fill in scratches/gouges, making your base like new again. ✨

Do you service snowboards too?

Absolutely! All our tuning and waxing services are available for both skis and snowboards. 🏂
